# Circadian APP design principles


1. Minimalistic and intentional — clean, spacious, easy to scan. Nothing competes for attention.
2. Clarity is care — every screen should feel obvious. No instructions needed, no second-guessing.
3. Graceful when things go wrong — if a session drops or something breaks, the app recovers smoothly and keeps the patient confident.
4. Light and uplifting — the experience should leave patients feeling better than when they started. Bright, warm, encouraging.
5. Works for everyone — generous touch targets, readable text, intuitive for any comfort level with technology.
6. Invisible motivation — gamification is structural, never performative. Progress is felt through visual rhythms (filled checkmarks, consistent logs, growing charts) rather than announced through badges, points, or celebratory language. The patient sees evidence of their own consistency — they draw their own conclusion. No "Great streak!" banners. The streak speaks for itself through six blue checkmarks in a row.
7. Respect over reward — the app never talks down to the patient or performs enthusiasm on their behalf. Tone stays calm, factual, and warm. Motivation comes from the patient feeling organized and in control of their treatment, not from the app cheerleading.
8. Senior-first design — every element must pass a 60+ usability test. This means high contrast, generous sizing, plain language, and forgiving interaction patterns. If it works well for a tired 65-year-old cancer patient at 7 AM, it works for everyone.
